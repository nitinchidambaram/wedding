Getting here

Krčkovice is located in the Czech countryside about an hour from Prague, close to some sandstone towers of course :) The venue is a lovely campsite in a forest, close to a lake and nearby climbing.

If you're flying/taking a train from outside the Czech Republic, it's probably best to get to Prague first. Krčkovice is roughly an hour and half driving from Prague. If you won't have a car, let us know and we'll put you in touch with some of our friends who can give you a ride (in exchange for a beer or two ;)).

A Bit More Info

Dates

Our wedding celebration will happen on Saturday, June 6. We'll have the venue from Thursday evening (June 4) until Monday morning (June 8). The official ceremony (i.e. doing paperwork at the town hall) is planned for Friday. You're welcome to join us for as long as we have the venue (but please note that we won't have food/drinks for everyone except on Saturday). The plan is to go climbing somewhere nearby on Sunday if the weather is good and spirits are high :)

After the wedding

On Monday, we plan to drive to Adršpach and spend a few days there climbing. You're more than welcome to join us for some climbing. The sandstone towers there are huge and the climbing is pretty adventurous! Absolutely worth going there as a climber if you've never been.

Accommodation

Krčkovice is a charming little village, which unfortunately means that there aren't a ton of options for sleeping. The campsite has tents which you can sleep in (bring a sleeping bag and sleeping mat if you plan to do that). Alternatively, there's room at the venue to put up your own tent or sleep in your car. If you'd rather not camp, there is a B&B nearby and three cottages as part of a nearby campsite . Please let us know in the RSVP form if you'd like us to reserve one of those options for you.

Weather

The weather should be pleasant during the day in June, but the evenings might get chilly. Bring some warm clothes for the evening/night. A rain jacket may come in handy as well (although we certainly hope not!)
